Experimental Psybient / Global Music & Art

2016-11-08 09:21
Location – Bangalore, India. Mind Map is a group of artists from different genres of art who have come together to map people’s mind with Experimental Psybient music. Mind Map sound scape involves Rhythmic melodies to psychedelic rare instruments. “We are in the process of crossing barriers and borders genres of art to unite it under one roof Global music / Global art”. At present Mind Map live performances include Live music, Live painting & Flow art (Fire & LED Poi) The present lineup includes • Preetham G Raj – Synth / arrangement / lead vocals • Avinash BN – Bass Guitar • Psy Didge / Abhijith – Didgeridoo & backing vocals • Thomas Thomson – Violin • Chirag – Hand Pan • Ashwin – Flow art / Poi • Kiran Singh / Trinetra creations – Art work & live painting Genre of Music we belong to Experimental Psybient Music, that is the genre that we belong to. Our music is inspired by nature – a clichéd statement, but honestly all artists who are part of this clan are inspired by nature and that is one of the reasons that we are together. We would all agree to the fact that natural sounds are very ambient. In our journey of music, we realized that natural sounds also have a psychedelic feel. Like if you close your eyes lying down on a beach, you start to drift with the sounds of the waves. As a band we try to replicate those natural sounds which resonate with our innate frequency. In that process we experiment with a lot of instruments and improvise with conventional ones. Hence the name Experimental Psybient Music. About the EP tracks (Which is on air on your channel) So it is not a collective, though, the tracks transcend musically. We have 3 tracks, all of which are precursors to full length albums that will follow Actual Instinct: This is our first track, which talks about the actual instincts that all of us have in common, which is to survive. We have clouded our mindset with materialistic and possessive intent, where as we should just let go all of it. Sense the actual instincts within each one of us. Alien Planet: The second track has a philosophical thought. We as a generation have been very obsessed with Aliens and the prospect of finding Aliens. However we are not realizing the fact that we ourselves are also Aliens in this universe for someone else. Mean Man: This track highlights the exploitation of nature by Man. It symbolizes Nature as a Mother who nurtures the Man, but the Mean Man fails to understand its importance. The track finally ends with Mother Nature showing its fury to the Mean Man who is belittled in front of the power of this universe. These are the 3 tracks which we have in our EP and as you see, all of them have different story but the same theme, Inspired by the Universe! About the Next Release We are working on full length album and is scheduled to release in end of 2016. Keep watching our space for more information. Plays Gone Wild

2016-10-27 17:18
(1) What is our project? Our AfrikaBurn project and our default world projects are, in fact, one and the same thing: We facilitate designing, building and performing in such a way that kids learn how to make *real* theatre. (Pukka is a Hindi word that means “genuine”.) Pukka Theatre wants to help Cape Town’s kids - and the kids at AfrikaBurn - develop skills that they can use in the big bad world, all the while encouraging them to play! We describe our specific project at AfrikaBurn in more practical terms in the planned activities box. (2) What is the story behind our project? *OUR MOTIVATION - In general* We realise that many of Cape Town’s kids experience harsh realities and extreme setbacks at a very young age. Parents leave or pass away. Kids witness violence. They witness (or suffer) domestic abuse. Scientific research shows that children are naturally resilient in the face of these setbacks, unless crucial parts of their cognitive development get stunted, and provided that they have ties with competent and caring adults. Pukka’s people are motivated to keep the *super important* developmental juices flowing so that our kids may have a better chance at overcoming the adverse circumstances that they face. *OUR MOTIVATION - at AfrikaBurn* We want to enable kids at AfrikaBurn to make a real contribution to the Burn. The workshops and performances we plan to facilitate are not nanny-ing activities that are fun for the kids but aimed at cutting the parents some slack so they can go party. We want to facilitate learning and contribution while having fun. *OUR MISSION* Our mission is to provide primary school children with a world-class theatre-making journey. Whether they are set in the Tankwa Karoo or in the Bo-Kaap or at the Oranjezicht City Farm Market, our theatre programmes target the development of the following skills: teamwork and communication, self-regulation, public speaking and performing, spatial problem-solving, bodily awareness and mindfulness, technical skills, marketing skills and - of course - creative, aesthetic expression. These skills are important contributors to resilience. Being resilient is all about seeing the power of sustained, focussed effort, trusting teamwork and believing in the magic of making things (happen)!
Plays Gone Wild - by Pukka Theatre Think about what your favourite grown-ups did when you were a kid. Which ones said or did something that changed your world. Or think about the ones you wished had said or done things differently. This is your chance to reconnect with your childhood wishes and adventures, to tap into the messages or actions that opened up your world as a kid and share that with others. We're a group of theatre makers and enthusiast from Cape Town (pukkatheatre.com). We aim to use the last four days of the Burn to help 7 to 12-year-old kids to build big theatre puppets - wild animals! - from scratch. The kids will then get to "perform" releasing these animals from our Stage Cage and into the AfrikaBurn wild. We will help the kids to parade the animals around the inner circle. We don’t tell children what to do or how to do things, we try and help them to solve the puzzle. Through asking questions and encouraging them to try things, we help them to learn as they progress. We praise effort, not ability or talent. The best is seeing a shy kid with low self-confidence figure something out, and then another and then another, all the while making something new, up until a point where they have enough confidence to help their peers and take pride in what they have made. By helping us at the Burn you get to facilitate that magic. We need volunteers to help with: (1) Setting up our Workshop Space and building our Stage Cage (on the Tuesday and the Wednesday) (2) Facilitating the kids activities (3) Making sure that kids get back safely to their parents' camp (4) Cleaning up

Puzzled?

2016-10-19 12:08
Puzzled? is our simple gifting project and experiment for Afrikaburn 2017. In short, we will create a puzzle from stainless steel (or brass). The puzzle pieces will be made into pendants, which will be gifted at the 2017 Burn. There will be approx. 500 pieces to be handed out. The aims of Puzzled? are to… • Meet new people and have fun • Do something interesting and different for Afrikaburn 2017 • See how much of the puzzle will be completed by the end of the 2017 Burn • Gift puzzle pendants to fellow Burners
We will need to collaborate with some camp who has a permanent "manned" presence. Burners will bring cardboard puzzle pieces to a fixed location, where they will exchange them for metal puzzle pendants. A name and photo will need to be taken. A theme camp such as "Burning Mail" would fit perfectly. The plan is to approach this subject early next year

Skydiving / parachute displays never fail to entertain and inspire. As per last year, we can land accurately in pre-determined landing areas with orange smoke indicating the wind speed and direction in the landing area. We will be preforming a number of skydiving / parachute displays at Afrika Burn Play. Any project that would like to collaborate with us in anyway is welcome to get in touch.

The Naked Spectrum

2016-11-04 17:37
The Naked Rainbow for AB 2016 was a huge success. The 'paparazzi' loved us. It was an incredibly liberating process for each colour. So we decided to make it bigger and evolve into The Naked Spectrum. The Naked Spectrum is a proclamation of radical self-expression: of stepping outside your clothes and into the liberating process of body painting yourself (or getting from a friend) a solid colour chosen from the rainbow. It is as deep a personal process as it is a collective one. To saunter through the playa as a walking artwork. To create a human formation which will be captured through the use of aerial photography with the collaboration of a licensed drone photographer - creating some kind of naked colourful history.
We are looking for volunteers to take part in The Naked Spectrum : a colourful naked performance artpiece that will come alive for one afternoon on the playa. This requires volunteers to dress themselves in one of the colours of the rainbow using body paint. The volunteer is free to hang onto their panties, if they must! After a stroll as a moving spectrum, we will gather at a specific location to create a human formation of which aerial photographs which will be taken - making some naked colourful history on the playa!

The Organ Player

2016-11-06 18:05
"The Organ Player" - sculpture - is a 7 meter high tower comprised of two sections. The top 4 meter section is a bundle of 7 organ pipes that are tuned to the notes of the 7 Chakras, Bb Eb F# C G D A, where the A = 432hz (Crown Chakra). The lower 3 meter section is a mild steel base for the organ pipes, containing 7 "fuel rods", one suspended beneath each pipe. Stainless steel cables support the structure, like an fm tower. Around the circumference of cable anker points on the ground, is a 1m high flammable hedge, to cover the cable ankers and provide light for the sculpture. A smaller fire beneath the tower creates the updraft that plays the organ.
The sculpture is like a giant camp fire, so the performance space would surround it. I have yet to test it but I imagine the organ pipes will be quite loud, so I imagine drums and instruments with good projection would be best. Silent performers and dancers would add a wonderful visual aspect, back lit by the fire that surrounds the sculpture in a ring, approximately 8m diameter.

The Portrait Exchange

2016-10-24 19:02
Fuelled by the belief that everyone can draw, the performance is a game that is played between the artist Anthea Moys and any willing player who will sit opposite her. The rules are that they both have to draw each other in one minute, but are not allowed to look down at the page, putting them both at a disadvantage. We then both sign and date the drawings and exchange them. As soon as I have received my portrait of myself I will then attach it with pegs to the rig beside me. Eventually this space fills up with all of the different portraits of myself drawn by all the participants over time. It is hoped that one day I will create a book and exhibition made up of all the portraits I have exchanged. The performance is about playing a game together with uncertain results, its about exchange and participation in bad drawings! Duration usually 3 hours, negotiable. The performance has been performed at the Creative Womxn conference August 2016, Infecting the City June 2016, Transitions exhibition May 2015, GIBS Business School May 2015 and at the Turbine Art Fair 2015. See here for more: http://antheamoys.com/portfolio/the-portrait-exchange/
Come draw! Come participate in the portrait exchange game!
